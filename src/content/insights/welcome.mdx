For two decades my writing on Bangladesh's ICT and telecom policy has lived inside the constraint of the op-ed page — 800 words, a single argument, an editor's headline. That format has its discipline. But it leaves a lot on the cutting-room floor.

This space exists for the longer form. The half-formed argument that needs to be tested in the open. The technical detail that an op-ed page won't carry. The follow-up to a piece in *The Daily Star* or *The Financial Express* that I've been wanting to write for a year. And the occasional cross-disciplinary essay that reaches outside telecom into energy, capital markets, or industrial policy — because none of these fields actually live in isolation.

The conviction that runs through everything here is the one I have been arguing in print since 2013: **ICT and IT-enabled services can be the next RMG for Bangladesh.** Garments built the country's last forty years. Software, services, and the digital economy will build the next forty — if our regulation and our skilling and our capital architecture catch up to the ambition.

This is where I'll work some of that argument out in public. Comments are off; the email at the bottom of every page is on.
