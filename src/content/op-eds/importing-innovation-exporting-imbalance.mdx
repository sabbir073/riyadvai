For two decades, Bangladesh has imported the symptoms of innovation — devices, platforms, frameworks — while exporting the imbalance: low-margin labour without IP, talent without equity, capital flight without offsetting digital exports.

This piece argues that the country's next great export engine — its next RMG — must be ICT and IT-enabled services, and the regulatory architecture has to be redesigned to enable rather than tax that ambition.
